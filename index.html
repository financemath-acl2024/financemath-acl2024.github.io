Overview of FinanceMath.

Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated impressive problem-solving capabilities across various domains, but their ability to handle knowledge-intensive mathematical reasoning in specialized fields remains understudied. To address this gap, we introduce FinanceMath, a benchmark designed to evaluate LLMs' performance in knowledge-intensive mathematical reasoning within the finance domain.

FinanceMath comprises 1200 examples covering a wide range of finance subareas, with 40.2% of problems requiring interpretation of tabular data. Each problem is accompanied by expert-annotated, Python-formatted solutions and a comprehensive knowledge bank of 864 financial terms and concepts.

We conducted a comprehensive evaluation of 44 prominent LLMs from 16 organizations, including math-specific, code-based, and mixture-of-experts models. The best-performing GPT-4o model achieves an overall accuracy of 60.9% using Chain-of-Thought prompting, significantly outperforming other models but still falling short of human expert performance (92%) by 31.1%.

Our analysis reveals that integrating domain-specific knowledge into prompts consistently improves LLMs' performance. This gap underscores the critical role that FinanceMath will play in advancing LLMs' capabilities in knowledge-intensive problem-solving, particularly in specialized domains like finance. We further explore various knowledge integration strategies, providing valuable insights for future research in enhancing LLMs' abilities in complex, domain-specific reasoning tasks.
